Opossum

Who are they?

Opossums, North America's only marsupials, are fascinating creatures. With their sharp senses, varied diet, and unique reproductive system, they've thrived in the wild. Discover these resilient, adaptable animals.

Reproduction

A female opossum gives birth to helpless young as tiny as honeybees. Babies immediately crawl into the mother's pouch, where they continue to develop. As they get larger, they will go in and out of the pouch and sometimes ride on the mother's back as she hunts for food.

Scavenger behaviour

Opossums are scavengers, and they often visit human homes or settlements to raid garbage cans, dumpsters, and other containers. They are attracted to carrion and can often be spotted near roadkill. Opossums also eat grass, nuts, and fruit. They will hunt mice, birds, insects, worms, snakes, and even chickens.
